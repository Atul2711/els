(i) Syllabus

The Europe Language School has its own syllabus which establishes the appropriate foundations for the teaching of universal, well-spoken foreign languages, while respecting the variety found among the diverse speaking communities. Teaching is done in a flexible manner, with the aid of the most up-to-date technology. The Institute is currently adapting its courses to be consistent with the levels established by the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR).

An initiative of the European Council, the CEFR aims to achieve a greater degree of uniformity and transparency in the study of foreign languages, among member nations of the European Union. This serves to increase mobility within member nations, facilitate mutual recognition of language certificates, and promote cooperation among educational institutions from different countries. The Framework defines the levels that determine linguistic capability in areas such as communicative proficiency, active and receptive skills, and linguistic proficiency with specific ends.

The CEFR divides language learners into three large categories which, in turn, are subdivided into six levels:

    A. Basic User

    • A1 (Breakthrough)
    • A2 (Waystage)

    B. Independent User

    • B1 (Threshold)
    • B2 (Vantage)

    C. Proficient User

    • C1 (Effective Operational Proficiency)
    • C2 (Mastery)

(v) EXAMS

  • Taking photographs and recording videos during the class without the permission of the person concerned and that of tests, assignments and examinations is unacceptable behavior and will lead to the expulsion of students with no fee refunds.
  • Students will have the opportunity to revise their final exam and be informed of the marks received during the course. Following this revision, the instructor will grade the students after consulting the Head of Studies in all such cases where there may be any doubt. Marks published in the result of Europe Language School will be considered official and, therefore non-negotiable. No student or their relatives will be attended to subsequently, for queries regarding the result.
  • Exam dates are fixed. A student who cannot appear for the exam on the scheduled date due to a justified reason may:
    • Send an email to : europeancouncil1@gmail.com , attaching documentation to justify his/her absence.
    • In case the petition is approved, the student can apply for a new date at least 12 working days prior to the official exam date.
    • The rescheduled exam date will always take place within the 5 working days.

(vi) DIAGNOSTIC EXAMS

  • Before registering for any language class in our center, a diagnostic exam is required for the following cases:
    • Students who have studied desired learning language in the past
    • Returning students of ELS (after 3 months for A1 to B1 levels and 6 months for B2 to C2 levels).
    • Students with no formal language training (but can speak the language).
    • Students with prior knowledge of desired learning language who want to enroll for an online course (AVE).
    • Diagnostic exams cost INR 800. This amount is to be paid at least two days in advance and is deducted from the course fee upon enrolment. Students who absent themselves for the appointment will not be refunded the fee. The time period for the deduction of this amount, as well as the academic validity of the test is different.
    • The administrative validity of three months implies the deduction of the diagnostic test fee of Rs 800 (from the total course fee), counted from the day the payment is made.
    • The academic validity involves the duration of the level obtained after taking the diagnostic test. It is 3 months for levels A1, A2 and B1 and 6 months for levels B2, C1 and C2.
    • The results of the diagnostic exam are final. But they are by no means a certification of the level and are for our internal useonly. Validity of the results is 3 months for A1 to B1 levels and 6 months for B2 to C2 levels
    • Note : No diagnostic exam has to be given by Beginners, who don't have any knowledge of desired learning language.
